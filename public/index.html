Common questions

Things home cooks ask us.

What is a MEHKO permit?

A Microenterprise Home Kitchen Operation (MEHKO) permit is California's legal framework — passed as AB 626 in 2018 — that lets home cooks prepare and sell full meals directly to customers from a private kitchen. Unlike Cottage Food (which only covers shelf-stable items like bread and jam), MEHKO covers hot food, meat, dairy, and perishables. You're capped at 30 meals/day, 60/week, and $100,000 in annual gross sales.

Which counties allow MEHKO?

As of 2026, 17 counties plus the City of Berkeley have opted in: San Diego, Los Angeles, Riverside, Alameda, Santa Clara, Sonoma, Solano, Santa Barbara, Lake, Imperial, and more. Your county has to explicitly adopt an ordinance for the program to be active locally. Big holdouts: Orange, San Bernardino, Fresno. Coverage continues to expand.

How much does it cost?

Permit fees vary by county — typically $400–$1,000 per year. San Diego County has waived fees through May 2025. You'll also need a Food Manager Certification (~$15, ServSafe online) and, if your home uses well water, a water quality lab test (~$45). Stoveside's permit wizard is free. We only make money when you do.

How long does approval take?

Most counties approve MEHKO applications in 2–4 weeks after you submit your packet and pass the in-home kitchen inspection. Stoveside gives you a submission-ready packet at the end of the wizard, so the slowest part is usually scheduling the county inspector's visit.

Can I use DoorDash or Uber Eats?

No. AB 626 explicitly prohibits third-party delivery. Customers can pick up from your home, or you can deliver it yourself. This is actually a feature — you keep 100% of the order value instead of giving 30% to a delivery app.

Do I need to change my zoning or get a business license?

No on both counts. AB 626 explicitly prevents cities and counties from requiring zoning changes, home occupation permits, or land use permits for MEHKOs. You might need a regular business license (same as any self-employed person), but that's a separate, simple filing.

What does Stoveside charge?

The permit wizard is free. The marketplace charges a small take rate on completed orders to keep the lights on. No monthly subscription, no listing fees, no charge before you make your first sale.

Is my home kitchen going to be inspected?

Yes, once per year. The inspector checks food safety fundamentals — proper storage, separate sinks for food prep, handwashing, safe temperatures. Stoveside gives you a pre-inspection checklist so nothing's a surprise.
